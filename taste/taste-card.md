## Principles — what I consistently choose
- **Square icon buttons as the default** — Standardize button shapes/sizes to reduce visual clutter and improve hierarchy; square icon buttons chosen as the default for uniformity.
- **3-tier token architecture (primitive to semantic to component)** — Automate styling and keep themes aligned; primitive tokens are temporary scaffolding while transitioning to semantic defaults. Taste = systematized, not hand-tuned per screen.
- **On-canvas animation as feedback, not decoration** — Animate the specific node that changed and zoom to it when AI adds something. Motion earns its place only when it communicates what happened.
- **Mobile = clean view-only + AI chat, not a full editor** — Two journeys: create-quickly-to-expose vs. see-quickly (Figma/Miro view mode). Don't over-build mobile editing; prioritize viewing + AI chat.
- **Reduce surface area, remove redundant UI** — Remove obsolete controls; demote destructive/rare actions into menus. Fewer, clearer affordances over more.
- **Familiar mental models over novelty** — Reach for patterns users already know (VS Code, Figma view mode, Miro, Claude memory) so the interface feels learnable rather than clever.
